The Myth of Sisyphus

The Myth of Sisyphus is a 1942 philosophical essay by Albert Camus. The work can be seen in relation to other absurdist works by Camus: the novel The Stranger (1942), the plays The Misunderstanding (1942) and Caligula (1944), and especially the eassy The Rebel (1951).

History

It is speculated that the fall of France and the chaos it caused for the French had something to do with prompting Camus to write this book.

Summary

The essay is organized into four chapters and one appendix.

Chapter 1: An Absurd Reasoning

Chapter 2: The Absurd Man

Chapter 3: Absurd Creation

Chapter 4: The Myth of Sisyphus
